Introduction

Rivers operate as dynamic physical systems shaped by continuous changes in flow velocity, discharge, and depth. These physical conditions influence how aquatic organisms occupy certain habitats, as flow regulates substrate movement, food availability, and refuge stability (Chow, 1994). In the Philippines, the Marikina River exhibits noticeable fluctuations in water movement due to seasonal rainfall and watershed runoff, which significantly modify its hydraulic characteristics throughout the year (DENR, 2020). Such changes directly affe ct organisms living within the river, particularly invasive species th at thrive in stable or slow-flowing env ironments.

One example is the janitor fish (Pterygoplichthys spp.), an invasive armored catfish known to proliferate in Philippine ri vers. Reports from the B ureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR, 2019) indicate that these fish prefer d eeper, slow ermoving waters where physical stress is minimal.
