Introduction

The world is producing more and more waste, and much of it is not being recycled properly. Traditional methods of sorting waste by hand are slow, inaccurate, and can lead to contamination of recyclable materials. This research aims to develop a device that can automatically sort waste, specifically focusing on non-biodegradable items. By using a camera and an Arduino board, the device will be able to accurately identify different types of non-biodegradable waste, such as plastic, PET bottles, cans, and Tetra Packs. This will help to improve recycling rates and reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills.
