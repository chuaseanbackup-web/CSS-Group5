Objectives

1. To determine how low-cost imaging tools can help detect and quantify Xanthomonas campestris infection in okra.
2. To evaluate the accuracy of image-based tools compared to traditional visual assessments.
3. To assess the effectiveness of spatiotemporal mapping in showing the progression of infection over time.
4. To determine if image-based methods provide earlier detection of the disease than conventional methods.
5. To evaluate the feasibility of a low-cost image-based mapping system for disease monitoring in resource-limited settings.
